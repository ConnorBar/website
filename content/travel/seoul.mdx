did a week in seoul before my first time in taiwan for a study abroad

## Getting around

Seoul's subway system rivals Tokyo's for coverage and beats it for ease of use — English signage is excellent, and the Naver Maps app (better than Google Maps in Korea) works offline and gives accurate transit directions.

T-money card = required. Same deal as Tokyo's Suica.

## Food highlights

Korean food is already familiar to most people, but eating it *in Korea* is a different experience.

- **Gwangjang Market** — the best single food destination I visited anywhere on this trip. Bindaetteok (mung bean pancakes), mayak gimbap, and raw beef bibimbap. Go hungry.
- **Jongno 3-ga pojangmacha** — street food tents around the old part of the city. Best for late night after the subway stops.
- **Tongin Market** — the one where you buy coins and spend them at different stalls. Underrated.

## Bukchon Hanok Village

Go early. Very early. By 9am the Instagram crowds are out. Before 7:30am it's quiet, atmospheric, and you can actually absorb the architecture.

## A language note

I don't speak Korean, which was a first for me in Asia — I'm so used to at least having Mandarin characters to lean on. Korea uses Hangul exclusively, so restaurant menus were genuinely opaque. More humbling than Taipei, in a different way.

It made me appreciate how much passive literacy I'd built in Chinese characters. Something to keep working on.
