---
title: "Chinese Speech Recognition"
---

Mandarin tone recognition is a hard problem — four tones plus a neutral, all mapped onto the same consonant-vowel pairs, and the meaning of a word completely changes depending on which tone you use. The goal here was to build a model that could identify tones reliably from raw audio.

The dataset was 200,000 audio samples that needed preprocessing before any training could happen. The naive sequential pipeline was too slow, so I rewrote it with Python's multiprocessing module and cut preprocessing time by 81%, producing around 3 million train/val/test examples in the process.

For segmentation I used [CTC Forced Alignment](https://docs.pytorch.org/audio/main/tutorials/ctc_forced_alignment_api_tutorial.html) to automatically detect phoneme boundaries — this let me cut audio at the right places without manual annotation.

The model itself is a Multi-Task Learning setup: a ResNet backbone for feature extraction paired with an LSTM for sequence modeling. On top of the main tone classification task, I added Phonotactic Constraint Learning (using known rules about which sound combinations are valid in Mandarin) and Counterfactual Data Augmentation to make the model more robust to tone confusions.

Learned alot since then and I would change a lot if I redid it - planning to revist this project soon and overhaul it...
