title: "Legend of the White Snake: Story Mode"
---

While taking CHNS 341 (Traditional Chinese Literature) at Purdue, we read 白蛇传 — one of the four great Chinese folk tales. A white snake spirit takes human form, falls in love, and gets caught between the mortal world and the spiritual one. The story has been retold hundreds of times across dynasties: opera, film, TV drama, manga. Each retelling shifts the moral framing completely depending on when it was made.

Sometimes Bai Suzhen is manipulative and dangerous. Sometimes she's chaotic neutral. Sometimes she's just a person who is wildly misunderstood. The Buddhist monk Fa Hai usually sits on the opposite end of that spectrum — hero or villain depending on the version.

For the final project we had to do a creative interpretation of something we read. I kept thinking about how the story has no fixed interpretation — every generation rewrites it to say something different based on societal beliefs and trends at the time — and the numerous versions of the same key story reminded me of [Minecraft Story Mode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minecraft:_Story_Mode), a game where the choices you make shape what the story actually is.

So I made a storyboard pulling from the different versions, mapping out the common beats across most retellings, then added branching at the key decision points leading to multiple endings. The goal is to keep developing it into a full 2D RPG — you play through 白蛇传 as Xu Xian (Bai Suzhen's husband/victim depending) making choices that pull you toward different readings of the characters. Is Bai Suzhen a villain or a victim? Is Xu Xian a coward or a man caught between worlds?

Built in Godot. Is a passion project and till early so moves at whatever pace it moves at.
